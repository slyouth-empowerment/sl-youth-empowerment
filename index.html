Who We Are

A National Movement for Youth Change

SLYE is a comprehensive, five-year programme designed to transform the lives of 1 million young people through multi-sectoral interventions at national scale.

Sierra Leone's future is intrinsically linked to the well-being and productivity of its youth. With a significant portion of the population being young people, there is an urgent need to address systemic challenges including limited educational access, gender-based violence, unemployment, and lack of life skills.

SLYE builds on the proven success of programs by UNFPA, Mastercard Foundation, and IRC scaling them nationally with meaningful inclusion of both girls and boys as equal partners in change.

The Problem We Solve

Key Challenges Facing Sierra Leone's Youth

Sierra Leone's young people face intersecting crises that demand urgent, coordinated national action.

Gender-Based Violence & Inequality

Adolescent girls face high vulnerability to GBV, exploitation, and early/forced marriage perpetuating cycles of poverty and school dropout.

Educational Barriers

Quality education remains inaccessible for out-of-school youth and rural communities, especially girls facing harassment and distance barriers.

High Youth Unemployment

Most young people lack relevant vocational skills, financial literacy, and capital leaving them unable to engage in productive economic activities.

Limited SRH Access

Adolescents lack comprehensive sexuality education and youth-friendly health services, leading to high rates of teenage pregnancy and HIV/STIs.

Programme Activities

How We Deliver Change

Specific, community-rooted activities designed for maximum reach and sustainable impact.

1

Curriculum Development with MBSSE

Standardized life skills and CSE curriculum co-created with the Ministry of Education, adapted for local contexts and languages.

2

50,000 Community Mentor Training

Training teachers and community leaders to facilitate weekly sessions at Youth Hubs across all 16 districts.

3

Multi-Channel Information Dissemination

Radio dramas, social media campaigns, and mobile-based learning platforms to reach youth in remote areas.

4

Financial Literacy Integration

Embedding financial literacy modules within the life skills curriculum to equip youth with money management from an early age.

1

Demand-Driven TVET Partnerships

Vocational training in high-demand trades offered through partnerships with established technical and vocational training institutions.

2

Revolving Fund & Start-Up Grants

Revolving fund providing start-up capital and business mentorship to 50,000 young entrepreneurs across Sierra Leone.

3

Digital for Development Program

Training 100,000 youth in digital literacy, coding, and online freelancing to access remote work opportunities globally.

4

Job Placement & Internship Services

Connecting youth with employment opportunities through partnerships with local businesses and government agencies.

1

Physical & Virtual Hub Establishment

Creating 10,000 Hubs across all 16 districts both physical centers and digital platforms for remote youth access.

2

Solar-Powered Digital Equipment

Equipping Hubs with solar-powered computers and internet connectivity to ensure functionality even in off-grid communities.

3

Girls' Circles & Boys' Dialogues

Weekly peer-led sessions creating safe spaces for open discussion on SRH, GBV, and gender equality.

4

Mobile Health Service Clinics

Partnering with local health clinics to provide youth-friendly sexual and reproductive health services at Hubs.

1

National Gender Norms Campaign

A high-visibility campaign challenging harmful gender norms and promoting girls' education and women's empowerment nationwide.

2

Youth Policy Advocacy Training

Equipping young people with knowledge, skills, and platforms to lobby for policies that protect and advance their rights.

3

National GBV Strategy Support

Supporting the Government of Sierra Leone to develop and implement a comprehensive national strategy to combat GBV.

4

National & Regional Youth Summits

Annual summits bringing together young people, policymakers, and private sector leaders for high-level dialogue.

Accountability & Results

Monitoring & Evaluation Framework

A robust results framework tracks every indicator to ensure transparency, learning, and impact at scale.

Number of youth enrolled in and graduating from life skills and vocational training programs, tracked quarterly by district.

Number of youth-led businesses established and their 12-month survival and revenue growth rates.

Reduction in adolescent pregnancy rates in target communities, measured against national baseline data.

Increase in youth employment rates and average income levels among programme graduates.

Qualitative data on changes in community attitudes towards gender equality and sexual and reproductive health.

Evidence of youth engagement in policy dialogue, community leadership roles, and civic participation.
